Members assemble at 7:30 AM, singing and praising HASHEM before 8 AM at the mercy ground.

The Sabbath day activities begin with the blowing of trumpets. The Rabbi (Minister in charge) leads the assembly in brief prayer.

The assembly breaks into groups for a one-hour Torah study session. Children are handled by volunteer teachers.

#CREED#
## Our Creed

From the Torah, we see that God called the Nation of Israel with these faithful words: **"Hear, O Israel: The Lord is one Lord"** (Deuteronomy 6:4).

God commanded: **"I am the Lord your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of bondage. You shall have no other gods before Me"** (Exodus 20:2-3).

We read: **"Know therefore this day, and consider it in your heart, that the Lord He is God in heaven above, and upon the earth beneath: there is none else."** (Deuteronomy 4:39, 35)

This eternal truth was expanded through the inspiration of God upon prophet Isaiah: **"I am the Lord, and there is none else, there is no other god beside Me"** (Isaiah 45:5).

This sacred declaration was handed to Moses, the servant of God, for all Israel: **"He made known His ways unto Moses, His acts unto the children of Israel"** (Psalm 103:7).

By this pronouncement, God made Israel the custodian of His laws, which are His covenant, customs, and traditions for all mankind: **"He has revealed His word to Jacob, His principles, and laws to Israel, He has not done this with any other nation"** (Psalm 147:19-20 NLT).

Paul echoed this to the Romans: **"What advantage, then, is there in being a Jew, or what value is there in circumcision? Much in every way! First of all, they have been entrusted with the very words of God"** (Romans 3:1-2 NIV).

This law which the Lord gave to Israel is for all mankind: **"One ordinance shall be both for you of the congregation, and also for the stranger that sojourns with you, an ordinance forever in your generation"** (Numbers 15:15-16, 29; Exodus 12:49).

God Himself affirmed this while making the eternal covenant upon Mount Sinai: **"Neither with you only do I make this covenant and this oath; but with him that stands here with us this day before the Lord our God, and also with him that is not here with us this day"** (Deuteronomy 29:14-15).

Moses emphasized: **"The Lord made not this covenant with our fathers, but with us, even us, who are all of us here alive this day"** (Deuteronomy 5:3).

Peter, through the inspiration of God, delivered this same speech on the day of Shavuot: **"For the promise is unto you, and to your children, and to all that are afar off, even as many as the Lord our God shall call"** (Acts 2:39).

Our God came down upon Mount Sinai and spoke unto Moses, His servant: **"Behold, I come unto you in a thick cloud, that the people may hear when I speak with you, and believe you forever."** (Exodus 19:9)

**"And the Lord came down upon Mount Sinai, on the top of the mount: and the Lord called Moses up to the top of the mount; and Moses went up"** (Exodus 19:20).

Nehemiah testified: **"You came down also upon mount Sinai and spoke with them from heaven, and gave them right judgments, and true laws, good statues and commandments: And made known unto them your holy Sabbath, and commanded them precepts, statutes, and laws, by the hand of Moses Your servant"** (Nehemiah 9:13-14).

Stephen, the martyr, affirmed this fact: **"This is he that was in the church in the wilderness with the angel which spoke to him in the mount Sinai, and with our fathers; who received the lively oracles to give unto us"** (Acts 7:38).

God has given these laws to His people Israel, who documented the everlasting words of God—the Torah, in the Holy Scriptures. Through the pages of the Holy Scriptures, the code of life is unravelled, and this is our creed. Amen!

Through these faithful words, we shall remain on the right track to living a holy life: **"It shall be our righteousness, if we observe to do all these commandments before the Lord our God, as He has commanded us"** (Deuteronomy 6:25).

When Moses made an end of speaking all these words to all Israel, he said: **"Set your hearts unto all the words which I testify among you this day, which you shall command your children to observe to do, all the words of this law. For it is not a vain thing for you; because it is your life"** (Deuteronomy 32:46-47).

Paul spoke to the credit of the creed: **"As we said before, so say now again, if any man preach any other gospel unto you than that you have received, let him be accursed"** (Galatians 1:9).

Our brother, Jude, reminded us: **"Earnestly contend for the faith which was once delivered unto the saints"** (Jude 3).

The saints are: **"They that keep the commandments of God and the faith of the Messiah"** (Revelations 14:12).

Therefore: **"Let no one deceive you"** (Matthew 24:4), because the words of God are forever settled: **"Forever O Lord, Your word is settled in heaven: It is settled forever"** (Psalm 119:89).

May God bless us with the courage to affirm His everlasting stand and eternal words to all mankind and above all, do them, even in all our generations, Amen!

> Hatzak, hatzak, v'nit' chazek
> Be strong, be strong,
> Let us be strengthened. Selah. (Amen).

#HAVDALAH#
## Havdalah — The Separation

The Havdalah separates the holy from the ordinary, light from darkness, Israel from the nations, Shabbat from the six days of work.

**Wine**
Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who creates the fruit of the vine.

**Spices**
Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who creates varieties of spices.

**Candle**
Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who creates the lights of fire.

**The Separation**
Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who separates the holy from the ordinary, light from darkness, Israel from the nations, the seventh day from the six days of work.
